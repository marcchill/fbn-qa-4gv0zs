From Intern to Ninja — Privacy Policy

Last updated: 2 July 2026 · Contact: mark.childerhose@gmail.com

From Intern to Ninja is a browser game by a solo developer. During this playtest phase, the game records how play sessions go so the game can be improved. This page says exactly what is collected, how to say no, and how to reach the developer.

What the game collects

  • Gameplay telemetry — which rooms you enter, play time, deaths, level progress, and similar game events, tied to a random anonymous id (not your name, email, or device identity).
  • A self-chosen player handle — the name you type at the start. Use any playful name you like; it does not need to be your real name.
  • Coarse demographics (optional) — an age band and current job, asked once in the end-of-level survey. Every field can be skipped.
  • Feedback — the star rating and free-text answers you type into the end-of-level HR-360 survey.
  • An email address, only if you ask for contact — the survey has an optional "I'd like to speak to the manager" checkbox; if you tick it and type an email, that email is stored with your feedback so the developer can reply. It is never required and never used for marketing.

What the game does NOT collect

  • No account, no password, no real-name requirement.
  • No health, financial, or other sensitive personal data.
  • No advertising identifiers and no ads in this build.

How to opt out

The main menu shows the recording notice — press [N] ("Hard pass") to switch all recording off for your machine. Every survey question is individually skippable, including SKIP ALL.

Under-16 players

If the age you enter indicates you are under 16, identifying fields (handle, job) are stripped on your machine before anything is sent — only anonymous gameplay telemetry and optional feedback remain.

Where the data goes

Events are sent to PostHog (US region), a standard product-analytics service, under a project controlled by the developer. As with any web service, PostHog's servers process routine request data (such as IP address) to receive events; the developer does not use it to identify players. Feedback is reviewed by the developer (with AI assistance) to prioritize fixes.

Retention and deletion

Playtest data is kept while the playtest program runs and is reviewed at least yearly. To have your data deleted, email mark.childerhose@gmail.com with your player handle (and approximate play date); it will be removed from the analytics project and feedback log.

Changes

If data practices change (for example, when advertising is added), this page will be updated before the change ships, and the in-game notice will reflect it.
